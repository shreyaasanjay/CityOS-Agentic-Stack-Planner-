We're trying to streamline how our emergency department handles chest-pain patients, and I need help designing the coordination. An ER doctor sees the patient first — vitals, basic labs — and only after that initial workup does she decide whether the problem looks cardiac or pulmonary. If it's cardiac, she refers the patient to our cardiologist, and that referral is what kicks off the cardiac workup (ECG, cardiac enzymes). She also sends the patient for a CT scan; the hospital has a single scanner, so only one patient can be in it at any moment. One wrinkle: each doctor only sees results from their own tests — the ER doctor sees the general labs, the cardiologist sees the cardiac ones — so they have to explicitly send findings to each other before anyone can settle on a diagnosis. And nobody administers any medication until our pharmacist has checked every prescription, from either doctor, for interactions and sent back an approval or a flag. We're done when the diagnosis is made, the treatment plan is set, and everyone has wrapped up.
