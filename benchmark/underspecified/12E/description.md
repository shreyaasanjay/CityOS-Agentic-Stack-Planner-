We're trying to figure out how three chefs should work together in one kitchen to put out a three-course meal. One chef makes the appetizer, one the main course, one the dessert. The kitchen has a single oven and a single stovetop, and only one person can use each at a time. Cooking the base of the appetizer or the main course needs both the stovetop and the oven; the dessert base only needs the oven. Besides their own dish, each chef also makes a small extra for a colleague — no equipment needed, and they can do it whenever they like: the main-course chef makes a garnish and passes it to the appetizer chef, the appetizer chef makes a sauce for the dessert chef, and the dessert chef makes a glaze for the main-course chef. A dish only counts as finished once its base is combined with the component received from the other chef. Nobody has to wait to begin — all three can start right away — and we're done when all three dishes are complete.
